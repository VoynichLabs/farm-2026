This is what high tech meets cute tiny baby birds looks like.

The boss's desk is now a farm operations center: a Dell monitor running Claude Code, a Mac Mini M4 Pro (64GB) running Farm Guardian, a chick brooder under dual heat lamps, a USB camera pointed into the brooder for the nesting-box feed, and Pawel the Yorkshire terrier in a striped shirt supervising from his chair.

Farm Guardian hit **v2.11.0** this week. Every version — from the first YOLO detection pipeline to 4K alert snapshots to three-camera support — was built by Claude Opus in conversation with the farmer. The system now runs three cameras simultaneously:

- **Reolink E1 Outdoor Pro** (4K PTZ) watching the yard and sky
- **Samsung Galaxy S7** (restored after being declared dead — it was just discharged) on RTSP
- **USB camera** on the Mac Mini pointed into the brooder

The chicks have their own enrichment program: the Samsung S7 sits in the brooder playing content while they huddle around the screen. Whether they're learning anything is debatable. Whether they're warm is not.

This is the setup that will protect 26 birds across a Hampton, CT homestead — no cloud services, no subscriptions, all running on local hardware with AI built by Claude. The entire Guardian codebase, from detection to deterrence to dashboard, was written in conversations between a farmer and an AI.
