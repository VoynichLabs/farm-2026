# Under-Deck Bird Housing — AI-Designed, Never Built

This project started as a plan to enclose the space beneath the rear deck into permanent, predator-proof bird housing. Claude designed the entire thing: measured from photos, generated interactive 3D models, produced architectural elevation drawings and floor plans, and priced out a complete bill of materials.

In the end, we went with a prefab **Producers Pride Universal Poultry Pen** from Tractor Supply instead — faster to deploy, good enough for the flock's needs, and up and running within a day. But the AI design process was genuinely impressive, and worth preserving as an example of what Claude can do with agricultural planning.

## What Claude Designed

Starting from a single photo of the backyard, Claude:

1. **Measured the space** using a RAM 1500 truck as a scale reference in the photo
2. **Modeled the enclosure** as an interactive 3D volume you can rotate and explore
3. **Produced a full CAD drawing** showing the house, deck, and enclosure together
4. **Created architectural drawings** — south elevation and floor plan
5. **Priced a complete BOM** — 11 line items totaling $568 from Tractor Supply

All of this from a conversation. No tape measure, no site visit, no architect.

## Key Dimensions

- **Front face width (south):** 8 ft
- **Left depth (west/staircase side):** 9 ft
- **Right depth (east side):** 12 ft
- **Height at house wall (north):** 7 ft
- **Ground type:** Dirt/gravel
- **Shape:** Trapezoidal (not rectangular)
- **Total covered area:** ~168 sq ft

## Why We Shelved It

The under-deck design was sound, but practical reality won out. The **Producers Pride Universal Poultry Pen** was available immediately at Tractor Supply, deploys in a day, and gives the adult flock a secure outdoor space with room to free-range around it. The under-deck space would have taken weeks of construction for a similar result.

The real value of this project was proving that AI can do legitimate agricultural design work — from photo-based measurement to architectural drawings to materials pricing. That's the story worth telling.
