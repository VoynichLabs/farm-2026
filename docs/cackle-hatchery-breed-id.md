## Disambiguation Notes

1. **Multiple grey chicks** — R1 through R4, R6, R8 all appear grey/blue. These could be:
   - All Blue/Splash Cochins (most likely given reviewer reports of Cochin-heavy shipments)
   - A mix of Blue Cochins and Blue Polish (Polish would have visible head pouf even at day-old)
   - One or two could be Blue Sumatras from the Exotic order (clean legs, no pouf)

2. **Key physical checks to resolve IDs:**
   - **Feathered feet** → Cochin (most likely) or Sultan
   - **Head crest/pouf** → Polish, Sultan, Houdan, Spitzhauben, or Crevecoeur
   - **5 toes** → Houdan, Sultan, or Dorking
   - **All black including skin/legs** → Ayam Cemani
   - **Distinctive comb shape** → Sicilian Buttercup (cup-shaped)
   - **Naked neck** → Turken

3. **E5 vs E3** — Both are black/silver striped from above. Could be the same chick photographed twice, or two Silver Duckwing birds (one Phoenix, one Yokohama).

## Tracking — How to Confirm as They Grow

As the chicks feather out (6-12 weeks), the following features will confirm breeds:

| Feature | Appears by | Breeds it confirms |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| Feathered feet/legs | 2-3 weeks | Cochin, Sultan, Brahma |
| Head crest developing | 2-4 weeks | Polish, Sultan, Houdan, Crevecoeur |
| Long tail feathers starting | 8-12 weeks | Phoenix, Yokohama |
| Buttercup comb shape | 4-6 weeks | Sicilian Buttercup |
| Game-bird posture | 6-8 weeks | Aseel, Cubalaya, Jungle Fowl |
| Green/beetle sheen on black | 8+ weeks | Sumatra, Australorp |
| Silver lacing pattern | 6-8 weeks | Silver Laced Cochin/Wyandotte |
